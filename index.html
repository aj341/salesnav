☰ How to Create Personas — Step by Step

  1. Go to Sales Navigator Homepage
  2. Click the "Personas" tab in the left sidebar
  3. Click "Create Persona" (you can create up to 3 custom ones)
  4. Name it (e.g., "Marketing Hire - ICP1")
  5. Set the Function, Seniority, Job Title, and Geography from the values above
  6. Save — it's now available as a one-click filter in any Lead Search

★ Using Personas Effectively

Apply Persona + Account Filters Together ▼

When you select a Persona in Lead Search, it sets your lead-level filters. You still need to add Account filters (company size, industry, tech) on top. This combination is the most powerful search method — Persona handles "who," Account filters handle "where."

Use with Relationship Explorer ▼

When viewing a saved Account, click "Relationship Explorer" to see all leads at that company matching your Persona. This is especially useful for ICP 1 (Marketing Hire) where you're targeting specific roles within larger companies.

Monitor Lead Recommendations ▼

Once your Personas are active, Sales Navigator will proactively recommend matching leads. Check the "Recommended Leads" section weekly — these are free prospects you'd otherwise have to search for. Apply your Persona filter to prioritise the best matches.

Filter Conflicts to Watch For ▼

Important: Any lead filters that contradict your Persona's attributes will be overridden. For example, if your Persona says "Manager, Director" seniority but you manually add "Entry Level," the Persona's values win. If you need to temporarily search outside your Persona, deselect it first.

Pro Tips & Best Practices

Research-backed strategies for optimal Sales Navigator to Dripify results.

1 Right-Size Your Lists

Sweet spot: 500–1,500 leads per search.

  • Over 2,500? Too broad — add more filters or segment by geography
  • Under 100? Too narrow — remove a filter (usually tech or spotlight)
  • Break large lists into geo-based Dripify campaigns (e.g., Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane)

2 Technology Filter Caveat

Sales Navigator's "Technologies Used" filter is unreliable.

  • LinkedIn doesn't disclose how they detect tech stacks
  • Data goes stale as companies change tools
  • Use it as a Precision tier bonus, not a core filter
  • Better alternative: Use BuiltWith or Wappalyzer to build tech-filtered lists, then cross-reference in Sales Navigator

3 Spotlight Filters Are Gold

Prospects in Spotlight are 64% more likely to respond to InMail.

  • Changed jobs (90 days): New role = new budget, new tools, open to pitches
  • Posted on LinkedIn (30 days): Active users who'll see your Dripify touches
  • Shared experiences: Same university, same group — warm connection path
  • Use sparingly in Balanced/Wide tiers (they reduce volume significantly)

4 Boolean Best Practices

Write Booleans in a text editor, then paste into Sales Navigator.

  • Operators MUST be uppercase: AND, OR, NOT
  • Use quotes for exact phrases: "Head of Marketing"
  • Parentheses for grouping: ("CEO" OR "Founder") NOT "Consultant"
  • Think in title clusters, not single titles
  • Always add NOT exclusions to remove noise

5 Seniority & Function Gotchas

LinkedIn guesses seniority from titles — and often gets it wrong.

  • A CEO with 4 open experiences might be tagged as "Entry Level" on one of them
  • Always pair seniority with explicit job titles for reliability
  • Function filter is more reliable than seniority for filtering departments
  • For ICP 2 (Founders), rely on job title over seniority since many founders have quirky titles

6 Saved Searches & Ongoing Prospecting

Save every search you create. Revisit quarterly.

  • Saved searches auto-detect new matching profiles
  • Green number badge = new leads added since last check
  • Revisit and refresh Boolean strings quarterly as your ICP evolves
  • Duplicate searches and change only geography for easy segmentation
